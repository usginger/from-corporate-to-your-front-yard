## 🔍 Diagnostic Checkpoint Definitions

### 1. 📐 Spatial Balance Check (Geometric Alignment)
* **What We Audit:** The physical layout of the character inside the four-quadrant **Tianzigé (田字格)** box. 
* **Failure Red Flags:** Radicals crowding out of boundaries, poor left-to-right spacing ratio, or baseline drifting. Characters must show structural geometry.

### 2. ✏️ Mechanical Stroke Order (Neural Trajectory)
* **What We Audit:** The exact physical sequence of pencil movements. Strokes must travel strictly top-to-bottom, left-to-right, outside-to-inside.
* **Failure Red Flags:** "Sketching" characters backward, lifting the pencil mid-stroke, or using incorrect corner hooks. *If the stroke order is broken, the long-term reading memory lane is broken.*

### 3. 🧠 Active Recall (The Screen-Free Blank Page Test)
* **What We Audit:** The brain's capability to pull a character completely out of thin air onto a blank sheet of paper, purely based on hearing a verbal cue or meaning.
* **Failure Red Flags:** The student relying on pinyin drop-down menus, keyboard hints, or visual multiple-choice options to identify the character.
