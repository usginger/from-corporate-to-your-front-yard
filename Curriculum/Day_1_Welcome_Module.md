## 📐 2. Day 1 Tool Assessment: The Finger-Pressure Protocol

Before touching a pencil to a grid sheet, we align the physical mechanics. On Day 1, the coach will manually adjust the student's grip to meet the **3-Point Control Standard**:

1. **The Index Anchor:** The index finger must curve naturally over the top of the pencil, matching the thumb's counter-pressure without overlapping it.
2. **The Clearance Zone:** Fingers must rest exactly **one inch** back from the sharpened lead tip. This prevents the student from blocking their own line of sight and crouching forward.
3. **The Wrist Pivot:** The side of the hand must rest flatly on the table, acting as a stable anchor. The movement comes from the fingers and wrist pivot, never from the whole forearm.

## 🧠 3. Understanding Your Core Target Milestones

True literacy cannot be rushed, but it can be systemized. Over the next few weeks, your brain will transition through three distinct developmental phases:

* **Week 1–3 (Spatial Awareness):** Training the visual cortex to recognize character boundaries, center alignment, and perfect geometric proportions inside the grid.
* **Week 4–6 (Structural Connection):** Hardwiring advanced stroke trajectories and recognizing how basic radicals combine to form complex modern vocabulary.
* **Week 7–9 (Fluent Automation):** Achieving rapid active recall. Transitioning entirely away from phonetic pinyin hints to writing full, complex thoughts directly onto blank paper.
